大氣森林

Atmospheric Forest

Rasa Smite / 拉脫維亞 & Raitis Smits / 德國

《大氣森林》是一個沉浸式投影裝置，將森林、氣候變化和大氣之間的複雜關係視覺化。它是對Pfynwald——一個古老的阿爾卑斯山針葉林進行的為期三年的藝術研究項目的成果。樹木釋放出大量的揮發性有機化合物，我們可以感覺到它是森林的一種習慣性氣味。科學家們早就知道森林的芳香之間的聯繫。一些人認為，松樹林的強烈氣味表明氣候變化可以得到限制，而另一些人則認為，揮發性的排放可能使全球變暖更加嚴重。作品通過將樹木的揮發性排放和樹脂壓力的數據視覺化，揭示了這種複雜的模式。為了創建 《大氣森林》，科學激光掃描儀被用來掃描點雲中的Pfynwald。觀眾可以在虛擬森林的排放樹中穿梭，自下而上地觀察森林，並沿著樹幹的路徑遠上排放森林，體驗陸地生態系統和大氣之間的相互作用。

"Forest Atmosphere" is an immersive projection installation that visualizes the complex relationship between forests, climate change, and the atmosphere. It is the outcome of a three-year artistic research project focused on the Pfynwald, an ancient Alpine coniferous forest. Trees emit a significant amount of volatile organic compounds, which we often perceive as the habitual scent of the forest. The connection between the fragrance of the forest has long been recognized by scientists. Some believe that the strong scent of pine forests indicates a potential mitigation of climate change, while others suggest that volatile emissions might exacerbate global warming. The artwork visualizes the volatile emissions from trees and resin pressure data, revealing these intricate patterns. To create "Forest Atmosphere," scientific laser scanners were used to scan the point clouds of the Pfynwald. Audiences can navigate through emitting trees in the virtual forest, observing the forest from bottom to top and ascending through the emitting forest along the path of tree trunks, experiencing the interplay between terrestrial ecosystems and the atmosphere.

Rasa Smite / 拉脫維亞 & Raitis Smits / 德國

Rasa Smite 和 Raitis Smits 自 90年代中期以來便開始從事藝術、科學和新興技術跨領域的創作與研究，為拉脫維亞 RIXC 新媒體文化中心創始人，策劃RIXC藝術科學節主辦人及期刊出版。Rasa Smite於利耶帕亞大學教授新媒體藝術、拉脫維亞藝術學院副教授（2015）、瑞士應用科學與藝術大學「生態數據計畫」研究員（2017）。Raitis Smits 於2017 年起擔任紐約市立大學研究員。兩位兼為麻省理工學院客座教授。作品曾在 Ars Electronica Center、HeK（瑞士）、Van Abbe 博物館（荷蘭）、塔林 KUMU 博物館（愛沙尼亞）、斯德哥爾摩科技博物館（瑞典）、RAM 畫廊（奧斯陸）、KIM當代藝術中心、阿森納國家藝術博物館（拉脫維亞）等地展出。曾獲 Prix Ars Electronica (1998)、國家優秀文化獎（2016）殊榮。

Rasa Smite and Raitis Smits have been engaged in interdisciplinary art, science, and emerging technology research and creation since the mid-1990s. They are the founders of RIXC Center for New Media Culture in Latvia, and have organized the RIXC Art Science Festival and publications. Rasa Smite is a professor of New Media Art at the University of Liepaja, an associate professor at the Art Academy of Latvia (2015), and a researcher for the "Eco Data" project at the University of Applied Sciences and Arts Northwestern Switzerland (2017). Raitis Smits has been a researcher at The City University of New York since 2017. Both have also been visiting professors at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Their works have been exhibited at venues such as Ars Electronica Center, HeK (Switzerland), Van Abbe Museum (Netherlands), KUMU Art Museum (Estonia), Museum of Science and Technology (Sweden), RAM Gallery (Oslo), KIM Contemporary Art Centre, and the Latvian National Museum of Art, among others. They have received accolades including the Prix Ars Electronica (1998) and the National Award for Outstanding Contribution to Culture (2016).
